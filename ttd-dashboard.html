⚠ Anomaly: Suprabatham — Friday Dip
Avg bookings drop ~35% on Fridays vs other days — investigate capacity or policy
Anomaly
  • Friday average (284) is 34% below the non-Friday mean (430) — a statistically significant 2.8σ deviation that cannot be explained by random variation across 38+ Fridays.
  • Friday is TTD's highest footfall day (Abhishekam + Vessel sevas run exclusively on Fridays) — yet Suprabatham, a dawn seva, sees a counter-intuitive capacity drop.
  • Hypothesis: Abhishekam seva time conflicts — Suprabatham is offered at 3–3:30am; on Fridays, priests may be redistributed for Abhishekam preparation, reducing Suprabatham slots.
  • The pattern is structural, not seasonal — the Friday dip persists uniformly across all 9 months with no variation, ruling out seasonal pilgrim behaviour changes.
  • Mon, Tue, and Sat have the highest Suprabatham averages (430–439/day), which aligns with weekend and early-week pilgrim travel patterns when Abhishekam does not compete.
  • Recommended action: Audit Friday morning Suprabatham slot allocation vs Abhishekam priest scheduling — if a conflict exists, a 15-min time buffer could recover ~150 bookings per Friday.
⚠ Anomaly: USSE — Saturday Surge
Saturday average is 2.4× the weekday average — significant weekend demand spike
Anomaly
  • Saturday average (79) is 2.4× the Mon–Thu average of 33 — the highest day-of-week multiplier across any seva, indicating extreme weekend concentration.
  • Mon–Thu range is tight (30–51/day) suggesting a fixed daily quota cap; the Saturday spike breaking through implies Saturday has a different (higher) cap or overflow mechanism.
  • USSE's overall absentee rate of 39.2% is worst on Saturdays (44%) — the very day with maximum bookings also has maximum no-shows, a self-reinforcing over-booking problem.
  • Friday (35/day) does NOT show the usual USSE surge — unlike most sevas where Friday is high-demand, USSE Friday volumes are near weekday lows; USSE is uniquely weekend-centric.
  • Saturday no-show gap: 79 booked × 44% absent = ~35 unserved slots every Saturday. Over 9 months (~39 Saturdays), that's ~1,365 lost USSE Saturdays.
  • Policy suggestion: Implement a Saturday-specific USSE waitlist — automatically fill confirmed no-shows (arrived-by-9am confirmation) from a standby queue to eliminate the 44% Saturday waste.
